Diagonal Builder

The diagonals define the quadrilateral. Adjust their lengths, crossing angle, and split point — and watch which shape emerges. Every special quadrilateral is just a special set of diagonal rules.

The key idea: two sticks crossed at a point, with four endpoints connected in order, always form a quadrilateral. Changing how they cross — equal lengths? perpendicular? bisecting? — determines whether you get a square, rhombus, kite, or something else entirely.
